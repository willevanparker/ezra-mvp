# Ezra MVP

## Overview

Ezra is an AI-powered advisor designed to educate, qualify, and prioritize insurance agency acquisition opportunities.

The purpose of Ezra is not to replace traditional outreach. Phone calls, emails, LinkedIn outreach, referrals, and industry networking remain the primary methods of identifying agency owners.

Instead, Ezra serves as an educational and qualification layer between initial outreach and a live conversation with the acquisition team.

Agency owners can ask questions about selling their agency, valuation, succession planning, branding, employee retention, operational support, and growth opportunities. During these conversations, Ezra gathers information that helps determine whether the opportunity aligns with acquisition criteria.

---

## Problem Statement

Insurance agency acquisition teams spend significant time identifying and engaging agency owners.

Many owners are curious about selling but are not yet ready to speak directly with an M&A advisor. As a result, valuable opportunities can be missed or delayed.

Ezra provides a low-pressure way for owners to:

* Learn about the acquisition process
* Ask questions anonymously
* Explore valuation and liquidity events
* Understand operational and growth benefits
* Share information about their agency and goals

The acquisition team receives summarized insights that help prioritize follow-up efforts.

---

## Target Acquisition Criteria

Ideal opportunities generally include:

* Independent retail insurance agencies
* 50%+ commercial lines business
* $1M - $5M gross income
* Located in:

  * Georgia
  * Florida
  * North Carolina
  * South Carolina
  * Tennessee
  * California
  * New York
  * New Jersey
  * Other major U.S. population centers

---

## Key Differentiators

The acquiring organization's differentiators include:

* Not private-equity owned
* Long-term investment backed by insurance industry family offices
* Sellers retain their brand identity
* Employees are retained
* Existing management remains in place
* Opportunity for seller equity participation
* Administrative and back-office support
* Growth-oriented acquisition strategy

---

## Common Seller Questions

Ezra should be able to answer questions related to:

### Valuation

* What is my agency worth?
* How are agencies valued?
* What impacts valuation?

### Liquidity Events

* What happens during a sale?
* How much liquidity might I receive?
* What does a typical transaction look like?

### Branding

* Will my agency keep its name?
* Will customers know ownership changed?

### Employees & Management

* What happens to my staff?
* Will I continue running the agency?
* How much autonomy remains after a transaction?

### Growth & Operations

* How can the organization help me grow?
* What administrative burdens can be removed?
* What support resources become available?

---

## MVP Workflow

1. Agency owner receives traditional outreach.
2. Agency owner visits Ezra.
3. Agency owner chats with Ezra.
4. Ezra answers questions and gathers qualification information.
5. Ezra creates a conversation summary.
6. Ezra identifies overall opportunity alignment.
7. Acquisition team receives a daily report of new conversations.

---

## Information Ezra Should Capture

### Agency Information

* Agency Name
* State
* Commercial Lines Percentage
* Revenue Range
* Employee Count

### Owner Information

* Owner Name
* Contact Information
* Retirement Timeline
* Succession Planning Status

### Interest Indicators

* Valuation Interest
* Growth Interest
* Operational Relief Interest
* Liquidity Event Interest
* Transaction Readiness

---

## Daily Report

The system should generate a daily summary containing:

* New conversations
* Agency information
* Owner objectives
* Questions asked
* Opportunity assessment
* Recommended follow-up priority

Examples:

### High Priority

Strong alignment with acquisition criteria.

### Medium Priority

Potential future opportunity. Worth monitoring.

### Low Priority

Limited current alignment, but information retained for future reference.

No conversations should be discarded.

## Success Criteria

The MVP is successful if:

1. Agency owners engage with Ezra.
2. Ezra answers common acquisition-related questions effectively.
3. Ezra captures useful qualification information.
4. Acquisition team members find the daily summaries valuable.
5. Ezra helps create warmer and more informed follow-up conversations.
